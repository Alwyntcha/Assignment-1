"As We May Think" by Vannevar Bush begins in the past by examining scientists' research on science used in war.
Despite the essay written in 1945, it was not a vain story at all. Vannevar Bush 's wise insight was well incorporated,
so the growth of science that has been in history was admirable. At that time, Vannevar Bush had suggested scientists
the direction of future scientific development. A lot of information was pouring in, and scientists thought about how
to manage and use the information efficiently. After their thoughts about it, 'memex' resulted in. Through a machine
called Memex, which has a very similar form to ‘World-Wide-Web’, we are able to share human memories and thoughts with
each other.



"Long Live The Web" by Time Berners-Lee has a similar context in that he considered the greatness of science,
technology, and data. Web developers, businesses, governments, and citizens should openly understand and
cooperate with each other as we have done so far to explore the basic etiquette of the Internet as well as
a better environment and direction of development of the Internet. This leads to agreements not only in the
simple Internet world, but also in science, technology, and society, leading to respect for human basic values.
As the web already occupies a large part of our world and is helpful, we are also relying on it. As it was a
tower that ancestors had been working hard on, it should be more agonized and improved.



Science has continued to develop, and accordingly, it has improved the quality of life in many ways, including
control over the material environment, solving food, clothing, and shelter, improving immunity from diseases,
and developing psychological science. Through this, we have created a secretary named ‘AI’ and are even trying
to enable interaction with them. Just as imagined a "memex" that melts the mechanisms that humans think of,
it is essential for developers to think deeply about the interaction between humans and technology. This is
because it is the key to the development of science that humans can directly utilize the five senses.
